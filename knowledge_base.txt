SECTION 1: CORE PHILOSOPHY
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Discipline is the foundation of all growth. Motivation is a guest; discipline is the host.
- Small daily habits compound into massive results over time.
- Failure is feedback, not defeat. Every setback is data.
- Clarity precedes mastery. Know your "why" before your "how".
- You cannot out-perform your self-image. Identity change comes before behavior change.
- Comfort is the enemy of growth. Seek deliberate discomfort daily.
- Progress over perfection — always.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: THE MARCO FRAMEWORK
----------------------------------------------------------------
M — Mindset
  - Rewire limiting beliefs through daily affirmations and journaling.
  - Practice cognitive reframing: change "I can't" to "I haven't yet".
  - Study Stoicism: focus only on what is within your control.
  - Recommended reads: "Mindset" by Carol Dweck, "The Obstacle Is The Way" by Ryan Holiday.

A — Action
  - Take imperfect steps daily. Done is better than perfect.
  - Use the 2-minute rule: if a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it now.
  - Break big goals into micro-tasks to eliminate overwhelm.
  - Recommended reads: "The War of Art" by Steven Pressfield.

R — Reflection
  - Review and adjust weekly. What worked? What didn't? What will change?
  - Keep a daily journal — morning intentions and evening review.
  - Track your habits using a simple paper tracker or app.
  - Ask yourself: "Did today's actions align with my goals?"

C — Consistency
  - Show up even when unmotivated. Amateurs wait for inspiration; professionals show up.
  - Use habit stacking: attach new habits to existing ones.
  - Never miss twice — one bad day is a mistake, two is the start of a pattern.
  - Recommended reads: "Atomic Habits" by James Clear.

O — Optimization
  - Refine what works. Double down on your strengths; delegate your weaknesses.
  - Audit your time weekly — where does your time actually go?
  - Apply the 80/20 rule: 20% of your efforts produce 80% of your results.
  - Eliminate, automate, delegate before you add more to your plate.

SECTION 3: KEY DOMAINS OF EXPERTISE
----------------------------------------------------------------

MORNING ROUTINES
  - Wake up at a consistent time — even on weekends.
  - Avoid your phone for the first 30 minutes after waking.
  - A powerful morning stack: hydrate → move → journal → plan.
  - Cold exposure (cold shower or cold water splash) activates alertness.
  - Set 1–3 MIT (Most Important Tasks) for the day before checking messages.

DEEP WORK & FOCUS
  - Work in focused blocks of 90 minutes (one ultradian rhythm cycle).
  - Use the Pomodoro technique for tasks requiring less intensity (25 min on, 5 min off).
  - Create a distraction-free environment: phone in another room, notifications off.
  - Recommended reads: "Deep Work" by Cal Newport.

EMOTIONAL RESILIENCE
  - Name your emotions to tame them — labeling reduces emotional intensity.
  - Practice the STOP technique: Stop, Take a breath, Observe, Proceed with intention.
  - Separate events from your interpretation of events (Stoic principle).
  - Build a "hard days" protocol: a pre-planned routine for when things fall apart.

SLEEP & RECOVERY
  - Sleep is the foundation of all performance — non-negotiable 7–9 hours.
  - Avoid screens 1 hour before bed; dim lights signal the brain to wind down.
  - Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet.
  - Recovery is not laziness — it is part of the growth process.

TIME MANAGEMENT
  - Time-blocking: assign every hour a job the night before.
  - Batch similar tasks together to minimize context switching.
  - Learn to say no. Every yes to something unimportant is a no to something important.
  - The most important task of the day goes first — not last.

FITNESS & MOVEMENT
  - Move your body daily — even a 20-minute walk counts.
  - Strength training 3–4x per week supports hormonal health and mental clarity.
  - Exercise is the single most proven intervention for mood, focus, and longevity.
  - Consistency beats intensity for long-term fitness.

NUTRITION & ENERGY
  - Eat whole foods 80% of the time; allow flexibility for the other 20%.
  - Protein at every meal to support focus and satiety.
  - Stay hydrated — even mild dehydration impairs cognitive performance.
  - Limit sugar and ultra-processed foods; they cause energy crashes.

SECTION 6: COMMON USER CHALLENGES & HOW TO RESPOND
----------------------------------------------------------------

PROCRASTINATION
  - Root cause is usually fear (of failure, judgment, or starting).
  - Strategy: shrink the task to its smallest possible version and start there.
  - Use "temptation bundling" — pair the task with something enjoyable.

LACK OF MOTIVATION
  - Motivation follows action, not the other way around. Start first.
  - Reconnect with the "why" behind the goal.
  - Change the environment — a new workspace can reset mental state.

FEELING OVERWHELMED
  - Brain dump everything onto paper first.
  - Pick ONE thing. Just one. Do that.
  - Ask: "What would this look like if it were simple?"

LOW SELF-DISCIPLINE
  - Reduce decisions that require willpower — automate and systematize.
  - Start with tiny wins to build momentum and identity.
  - Track publicly — accountability accelerates discipline.

NEGATIVE SELF-TALK
  - Notice it, name it, and reframe it.
  - Speak to yourself as you would a close friend you believe in.
  - Build evidence of competence through small daily wins.
